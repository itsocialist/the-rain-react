# THE RAIN CONTINUES
**Game Concept Overview v2.0 (Neo-Pluvial Update)**

**GENRE:** Survival Exploration
**PLATFORM:** PC / Console 
**PLAYERS:** Single Player 
**RATING:** Mature

> The flood took the streets. The sky never cleared. What’s left is yours to hold together — or let drown.

---

## 1. Concept

Fifteen years ago, the rain started and never stopped. A shift in ocean-atmosphere coupling — modeled on the real Carnian Pluvial Episode of 234 million years ago — restructured the global hydrological cycle. Everything below the thirtieth floor belongs to the water now. 

*The Rain Continues* is a systemic survival exploration game set across a single drowned city. Above the flood, players traverse a decaying skyline of improvised bridges to manage fragile rooftop communities. Below, they dive into submerged, lightless interiors to salvage medicine, tools, and relics. 

Every dive is a risk mapped to a physiological simulation. Every skyline crossing is a calculation against wind and crumbling infrastructure. Every decision feeds or starves the people clustered on the rooftops.

## 2. Tone & Aesthetic

> **Not post-apocalyptic. Post-adaptation.**

The world has reorganized. People survive, build, argue, and adapt. Tension comes from holding something fragile together, not from nihilistic despair. 

*   **Visual Palette:** Concrete grey, rust orange, algae green, deep brown. The sky is a perpetual, oppressive overcast. 
*   **Color Accents:** Vibrant color returns through struggling rooftop gardens, the eerie bioluminescence of the deep flooded zones, and the improvised glow of scavenged lanterns.
*   **Audio Landscape:** Rain is the constant, inescapable baseline. Above water: wind shear, creaking metal, synthetic tarps flapping. Below water: absolute silence, replaced by muffled currents, building groans, and your own labored breathing.
*   **No Guns:** Conflict is structural, social, and environmental. Antagonists are gravity, corrosion, time, the ecosystem, and the politics of scarcity.

## 3. Core Pillars

### I. Vertical Open World & Dual-State Traversal
The same city exists in two layers. The player's body is a system to manage, constantly shifting between a 3 m/s surface walk, 2 m/s swim, and high-drag subsurface movement. 
*   **The Skyline (Above):** Traverse a procedurally degrading network of rooftop bridges, ziplines, and skywalks. Manage stamina, grip stamina, wind exposure, and structural integrity during crossings.
*   **The Deep (Below):** Dive into submerged interiors (offices, subway stations) to scavenge. The deeper you go, the darker it gets, requiring a headlamp and careful management of air pockets trapped in flooded stairwells.

![Bridge Traversal](/Users/briandawson/workspace/the-rain-react/reference_assets/visual_target_bridge.png)
*Precarious traversal between structural peaks. Wetness and rust degrade grip reliability.*

### II. Deep Physiological Systems
The player isn't just a camera; they are a fragile biological framework. 
*   **Respiration:** Breath capacity is finite. Submerging starts the clock, while extreme exertion (swimming against currents) burns oxygen faster. Mid-game rebreathers require constantly degrading chemical filters.
*   **Thermal Regulation:** Constant rain induces hypothermia. A "wetness" state amplifies the cold penalty, eventually triggering tremors that reduce interaction accuracy. Extended exposure leads to "Fever States" that invert thermal needs.
*   **Contamination:** Water exists in Safe, Brackish, and Toxic tiers. Swimming in industrial runoff builds a "Bioaccumulation" toxicity meter requiring antitoxins, forcing reliance on crafted Filter Masks that reduce vision for safety.
*   **Fatigue:** Restoring short-term stamina costs long-term fatigue. Scavenged pre-collapse food restores energy but risks contamination.

![Grip Mechanics](/Users/briandawson/workspace/the-rain-react/reference_assets/visual_target_grip.png)
*Stamina and grip are granular, life-or-death resources when infrastructure inevitably fails.*

### III. Weather as the Ultimate Antagonist
Weather isn't a skybox; it’s an active, cyclical threat driven by a neural simulation model.
*   **Storm Surges:** Cyclical storms raise the waterline by meters, turning safe walkways into submerged hazards, drawing deep-water threats higher, and flooding borderline safe rooms.
*   **Lightning & Fog:** Lightning strikes exposed metal structures, making sky-bridges lethal during squalls. Post-storm humidity generates volumetric fog banks that reduce visibility to zero, heavily impacting navigation but aiding stealth.

## 4. The Drowned City: Biomes & Decay

The environment is an active participant. Structures aren't permanent.

*   **Infrastructure Decay:** Every building's structural integrity drops from storm events and player interactions (e.g., prying doors). Bridges collapse permanently. The city you explore on Day 1 will not be the same city on Day 50.
*   **Procedural Reclamation:** Nature reclaims spaces. Unexplored areas slowly accumulate fungal and root growths, blocking passages but providing new harvestable materials.

### Vertical Biome Zones
1.  **Rooftop Canopy (Above Water):** Vine-covered safe traversal zones, rain gardens, exposed to wind/lightning.
2.  **Mid-Rise Tidal (Fluctuating Waterline):** Tide pools, rusted office floors, unstable structures. Primary scavenging.
3.  **Street-Level Marsh (1-3m Depth):** Murky channels, thick vegetation, ambush points. Must navigate via raft or dangerous wading.
4.  **Submerged Commercial (3-10m Depth):** Intact interiors trapped in time. Bioluminescence, no natural light. Rebreather territory.
5.  **Deep Infrastructure (10m+ Depth):** The abyss. Subway tunnels, sealed vaults. Total darkness and extreme pressure.
6.  **Toxic Plumes (Variable Depth):** Industrial runoff zones blocking direct routes.

## 5. Ecosystem & Fauna

The flood brought new life. The fauna are part of the ecosystem, providing resources, hazards, and dynamic interactions.
*   **Canal Rats:** Swarm swimmers that contaminate food stores; pelts provide thermal insulation.
*   **Heron Sentinels:** Rooftop watchdog birds whose alarm calls alert hostile factions and larger predators.
*   **Tunnel Eels:** Ambush predators with bioluminescent lures, hiding in the dark of flooded offices.
*   **Spore Crabs:** Fungal-symbiote crustaceans in the marsh level; venomous but yield high-grade armor chitin.
*   **Murk Crawlers:** Apex predators of the street-level marsh. Highly territorial.
*   **Swarm Moths:** Attracted to the player's headlamp; obscure vision but indicate nearby medicinal fungal growth.
*   **Depth Lurkers:** Massive, sound-sensitive threats in the Deep Infrastructure tier. Rarely fully seen, only felt via sonar pings and muffled rumbles.

## 6. Items, Progression & Economy

Progression is organic and use-based. **There are no traditional skill trees.** You get better at scavenging by scavenging; you improve at engineering by repairing infrastructure. 

### Scavenging Economy 
Everything degrades. The world is wet and hostile to preservation. 
*   **Scrap Metal:** Rushes if stored wet. Used for construction. 
*   **Polymer Sheeting:** Resists UV but tears. Vital for waterproofing and shelter.
*   **Bioluminescent Fungi:** Dims after 48 in-game hours. Used for low-grade medicine and light.
*   **Chemical Precursors:** Highly volatile. Required for water treatment.

## 7. Narrative & Community Management

The narrative operates on three layers, grounded tightly to the player's core gameplay loop of diving and delivering.

### The Towers (Management Layer)
Rooftop communities rely on the player's salvage. You must assign rescued residents to farming, rain catchment, radio operations, and dive prep. 
*   **Politics of Scarcity:** Manage inter-tower trade and reputation. Favoring one tower with medical supplies might alienate another spanning a critical bridge route. 

### The Narrative Structure
*   **The Runner (Present-Tense):** Skyline missions building routes and juggling the immediate socio-political survival of the remaining factions (Keepers, Currents, Undertow).
*   **The Diver (Exploration):** Pushing deeper into the increasingly alien abyss, revealing the post-pluvial ecology.
*   **The Journal (Lore):** Found fragments from the early years of the flood, scattered in the deepest, most dangerous vaults. Decrypting the "Project Deluge" data forms the core lore pursuit, rewarding extreme dive risks with potent narrative payoffs.

> **The rain continues. What you do beneath it is the only thing that matters.**
